Root Cause Classification

The geographic signature determines whether a recall is necessary — and whether it needs to be national.

Environmental

🔥 Geographic Clustering Detected

Complaints over-indexed in specific climate zones. The component failure pattern correlates with heat, cold, salt, or humidity — not with production batch or model variant.

  • Regional service bulletin only
  • Enhanced component spec for affected climates
  • Proactive outreach to regional customers
  • No national recall required

Manufacturing Defect

⚠️ Uniform Distribution Detected

Complaints distributed proportionally to population across all regions. No climate correlation. The defect is in the design or production — affects all units regardless of location.

  • National recall required
  • Full production batch investigation
  • Supplier quality review
  • Design specification review
  • Full recall budget applies
Classification Algorithm
Step 01 — Measure

Calculate Stress Factor

For each climate zone, divide observed complaint share by expected share (population proportion).

stress = observed% / expected%
Step 02 — Match

Component-Climate Correlation

Verify the failing component is physically consistent with the climate pattern. Hot climates → cooling, seals. Cold → battery, fluids.

match = component ∈ climate_profile
Step 03 — Classify

Recommend Action

If stress factor exceeds threshold AND component matches climate, classify as Environmental and recommend regional fix.

if stress > 1.5 and match → ENV
