Why we exist

Behind every recall, people got hurt first

Every NHTSA complaint database entry represents a real person — a brake that failed, an engine that caught fire, a steering column that locked at highway speed. The data to prevent these tragedies has always existed. It simply was not being read with the right methodology.

🛡️

Protecting lives through early warning

Our research methodology detects statistically anomalous complaint clusters in federal data — months before NHTSA opens a formal investigation. Earlier detection means earlier intervention. Fewer accidents. Fewer injuries. Fewer fatalities.

This is not an algorithmic exercise. It is applied safety science with a direct human impact.

📊

Translating risk into financial intelligence

Safety risk that is not yet priced by the market is the definition of alpha. Our research quantifies unpriced automotive exposure in dollar terms — expected loss, worst-case VaR, and urgency tiers — in the language that risk managers and credit analysts understand.

We do not predict. We measure what is already present in the data and price it honestly.

Our methodology is transparent and reproducible. We apply severity-weighted Poisson cluster detection across 85,000+ federal complaint records. Every signal carries a stated precision, a confidence tier, and a Monte Carlo loss distribution.

We do not report inflated accuracy figures. Our historical precision is 33% — one in three signals leads to a confirmed regulatory action within 365 days. The other two-thirds represent unpriced risk that may still materialize on a longer horizon.

We call this honest research. Our clients receive signals with explicit uncertainty bounds — not false certainty.

About

The research team

Dr. Shlomi Sela

Founder & Chief Scientist — Ph.D. Machine Learning & NLP

RecallVantage Research was founded on a straightforward observation: the federal complaint database maintained by NHTSA contains early signatures of dangerous vehicle defects. The statistical methods to detect these signatures — adapted from epidemiological surveillance — are well established. The application to automotive safety risk was not.

Dr. Sela brings over 40 years of applied analytical experience across engineering, machine learning, and large-scale data systems. The methodology draws on Poisson cluster detection, NLP severity scoring, and temporal backtesting — producing signals that are statistically grounded, financially quantified, and operationally actionable.

RecallVantage Research is the first of several research lines. Future coverage will extend to vehicle telematics risk and warranty reserve analysis.

"The intelligence was always in the data. The question was whether anyone was reading it with the right methodology — and the right sense of responsibility."
